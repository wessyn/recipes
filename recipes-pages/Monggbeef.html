Mongolian Ground Beef Noodles Recipe

Description

These Mongolian ground beef noodles have all the flavors you love in Mongolian beef, but in a simplified, more affordable version. Fragrant with ginger and garlic, in a balanced savory-sweet-salty soy and hoisin sauce, this dish can be on the table in a smidge over half an hour.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces spaghetti
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon pureed ginger
  • 2 tablespoons light brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 3/4 cup beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon rice wine vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon crushed red chili flakes
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons cold water
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds

    • Directions

    1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes.. Drain and run under cold water to stop cooking. Set aside.
    2. Meanwhile, crumble ground beef in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook for 4 minutes. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more. Drain any excess grease.
    3. Return beef to skillet. Add ginger, brown sugar, soy sauce, hoisin, broth, vinegar, and pepper flakes.
    4. Add cornstarch and cold water to a small jar with a lid. Cover and shake until combined and mixture is a thick slurry. Pour over beef mixture.
    5. Cook over medium-high heat until thickened, about 3 minutes. Stir in reserved spaghetti and cook 2 minutes more. Turn heat off and let sit for 1 minute.
    6. Sprinkle each serving with green onion and sesame seeds.
